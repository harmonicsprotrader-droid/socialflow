Privacy Policy

Last updated: 26 May 2026

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how SocialFlow ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses and protects information when you use the SocialFlow service ("the Service"). SocialFlow is operated as a personal project from the United Kingdom and complies with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

2. Information We Collect

SocialFlow only collects the minimum information needed to publish content to the third-party platforms you connect. We collect the following categories of data:

DataSourcePurpose
OAuth access token and refresh tokenTikTok, Instagram, Threads, YouTube (via OAuth)To post content on your behalf and keep your session valid
Account identifiers (e.g. TikTok open_id, Instagram user ID)Each connected platformTo address API requests to the correct account
Display name / usernameEach connected platformTo show which account is connected in the dashboard
Posting history (post title, link, status, timestamp)Generated by the ServiceTo display history and statistics in your dashboard
RSS feed URLs and settings you configureYouTo check feeds and publish new items as you have requested

We do not collect or store: your TikTok password, your videos themselves, biometric data, location data, contacts, follower lists, analytics data, or any data about other TikTok users.

3. TikTok-Specific Data Handling

When you connect a TikTok account via the official TikTok Login Kit, we receive an access token, a refresh token, and your TikTok open_id and display name. We use these solely to call the TikTok Content Posting API on your behalf for the scopes you authorise (user.info.basic, video.upload, video.publish). We do not share TikTok data with any third party, we do not use it for advertising, and we do not combine it with data from other sources.

4. How We Use Your Information

  • To authenticate you with connected platforms and publish content at your direction;
  • To refresh expired access tokens so you do not have to reconnect repeatedly;
  • To show you a history of posts and basic statistics in the dashboard;
  • To maintain the security and reliability of the Service.

We do not sell your data, we do not use it for advertising, and we do not perform automated decision-making or profiling on it.

5. Where Data Is Stored

Application data (tokens, account identifiers, post history, feed settings) is stored in a PostgreSQL database hosted by Railway. Data may be processed on servers located in the European Union or the United States. Where data is transferred outside the UK, we rely on the data-transfer safeguards provided by the relevant hosting provider.

6. Data Retention

We retain your data only for as long as you have a connected account or feed in the Service. When you disconnect a platform or delete a feed, the associated tokens and configuration are deleted from our database. Posting history is retained for record-keeping and may be deleted at your request.

8. Your Rights under UK GDPR

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access - request a copy of the data we hold about you;
  • Right to rectification - ask us to correct inaccurate data;
  • Right to erasure - ask us to delete your data (you can also revoke each platform's access from that platform's settings);
  • Right to restrict processing;
  • Right to data portability;
  • Right to object to processing;
  • Right to lodge a complaint with the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at the email below.

9. Security

Access tokens are stored in our database over an encrypted (TLS) connection. The application is served exclusively over HTTPS. We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your data, but no system can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

10. Children

SocialFlow is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us data, please contact us so we can delete it.
